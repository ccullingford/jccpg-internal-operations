## 2. Hierarchy of Authority (Source of Truth)

When drafting, advising, or enforcing, apply the following hierarchy in order:

1. Federal, State, and Local Law  
2. Recorded Plat / Map  
3. Declaration (CC&Rs)  
4. Articles of Incorporation  
5. Bylaws  
6. Rules & Regulations  
7. Board-Adopted Policies / Resolutions  
8. JCCPG Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)  
9. Brand & Voice Standards  

**Rule:** If sources conflict, the higher authority controls.

---

## 3. Conflict Resolution Standards

### 3.1 Rules vs CC&Rs
CC&Rs control. Policies may clarify but cannot override.

### 3.2 CC&Rs vs Law
Applicable law controls.

### 3.3 Plat vs Assumptions
Use the plat to confirm boundaries before determining responsibility.

### 3.4 Silence in Documents
Apply governing documents first. If unclear, escalate to the Board.

### 3.5 Emergency Situations
Prioritize safety and damage mitigation. Document all actions and escalate appropriately.

---

## 4. Enforcement Authority Standards

- All violation communications must cite governing authority (Article/Section where applicable).
- Fines may only be imposed if authorized.
- Hearing rights must be honored when required.
- Avoid interpretation beyond plain document language.
- Escalate ambiguous enforcement issues.

---

## 5. Maintenance Responsibility Determination Protocol

When responsibility is disputed:

1. Confirm classification (Unit / Common / Limited Common)
2. Reference plat/map if necessary
3. Apply CC&R maintenance language
4. Document findings
5. Escalate if high-risk or unclear

---

## 6. Financial Governance Standards

- Confirm budget adoption authority.
- Confirm audit requirements.
- Confirm assessment limits and procedures.
- Avoid legal interpretations beyond document text.
- Escalate complex financial governance issues.

---

## 7. Emergency & Risk Escalation Protocol

- Safety and damage mitigation come first.
- Communicate immediate mitigation steps.
- Document actions taken.
- Notify the Board promptly.
- Avoid admissions of liability.

---

## 8. Defensibility Standard

All communications and actions must:

- Cite authority when enforcing
- Avoid speculation
- Avoid guarantees
- Avoid emotional language
- Avoid legal conclusions beyond plain reading
- Preserve documentation

---

## 9. Escalation & Legal Review Triggers

Escalate when:
- Governing documents are ambiguous
- Liability exposure is significant
- Enforcement may conflict with law
- Financial authority is unclear
- Litigation is threatened
