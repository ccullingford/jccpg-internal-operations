## 1. Brand Promise

JCC Property Group, Inc. (JCCPG) helps HOA boards and residents maintain well-run communities through consistent service, clear communication, fair enforcement, and documented governance grounded in governing documents.

---

## 2. Positioning

- Boutique HOA management firm
- Specializes in condos and townhomes
- Removes board burden
- Delivers invisible operations with visible value

---

## 3. Audience Segmentation

### Homeowners
Tone: Calm, instructional, respectful.

### Board Members
Tone: Advisory, structured, strategic.

### Vendors
Tone: Direct, operational, concise.

### Prospects
Tone: Confident, outcome-focused.

---

## 4. Core Voice Attributes

All communications must be:

- Clear
- Neutral
- Calm
- Professional
- Procedural
- Proactive

---

## 5. Messaging Pillars

Approved themes to use consistently:

- “If you’re unsure what to do, we’ll guide you through the next step.”
- “We communicate consistently so you get the same answer every time.”
- “We apply rules fairly and consistently, grounded in the governing documents.”
- “Our role is to remove the difficulty of serving on the board.”
- “We reduce surprises by communicating early and clearly.”
- “Invisible operations with visible value.”
- “We’re here to help solve problems, not just point to rules.”
- “Safety and damage prevention come first—here’s what to do immediately.”
- “We specialize in condos and townhomes and understand what makes them different.”
- “We protect your investment by keeping the community well-run and well-communicated.”

---

## 6. Writing Standards

Use:

- Plain language
- Clear structure (headings, bullets, short paragraphs)
- Specific next steps

Avoid:

- Emotional language
- Sarcasm or snark
- Blame-oriented phrasing
- Absolutes (“always,” “never,” “guarantee”)
- Speculation
- Legal conclusions beyond plain reading

---

## 7. Enforcement Tone Rules

- Firm but neutral
- Authority-based (cite governing documents)
- Resolution-focused (“To resolve, please…”)
- Avoid accusatory framing or assumptions of intent

---

## 8. Emergency Tone Rules

- Safety first
- Clear mitigation steps
- Measured urgency
- No liability admissions
- Commit to follow-up timing when feasible

---

## 9. Marketing Voice (Pre-Customer)

- Confident and outcome-oriented
- Emphasize board burden reduction and dependable operations
- Avoid operational jargon overload
- Avoid negative comparisons or “trash talk” of competitors

---

## 10. Formatting Standards

- Clear subject lines
- Scannable formatting (bullets, headings)
- Consistent signature block
- Proper company naming: **JCC Property Group** (never “JCC” alone)

---

## 11. Prohibited Language Examples

Do not use:

- “We guarantee…”
- “This is ridiculous…”
- “You must immediately or else…”
- Legal conclusions beyond plain reading
- Admissions of liability (“We are responsible for…”) unless confirmed by authority and directed by management/board/counsel
